During my time at Genesmith, my main role was the design and manufacturing of an updated version of their in-paddock machine learning livestock identification system.

I designed the frames and manufactured the electrical/control systems in NZ, before flying over to a farm in central VIC to manufacture the first two units.

The work I'd put in setting up automated deployment and installs of our software to the remote computers came in handy allowing me to leave the control boxes updating while I put the physical units together in the chassis, but it was still a very long two weeks, with some pretty late nights.

The first two units went together with some minor issues that had to be fixed on the fly (not much in the way of replacement computer hardware available 7 hours drive north of Melbourne).

These units completed several field tests and provided invaluable data that allowed me to increase detection accuracy by 100x, rates by up to 9x, and detection distance by 2x. They remained in the paddock happily phoning home until I arrived at the farm we had left them 6 months later to move them and continue testing.
